- Menu quotations: Official menu sections are “Breakfast,” “Sandwiches,” “Fish,” “Balkan Specials,” “Soups,” “Pitas,” “Salads,” “Side Orders,” “Desserts,” and “Beverages.” Examples include “Five grilled beef sausages served with eggs”; “Premium Angus beef patty”; “Pan fried whole trout”; “Grilled veal and chicken schnitzel, veal kebab, four cevapi and small pljeskavica”; “Breaded Veal or Chicken breast hand-cut thin slices”; “Four grilled sausages made of veal, beef, and a little touch of garlic”; and “Poached apples stuffed with walnuts and chocolate.” The menu lists both Balkan items and burgers, omelettes, salmon, tilapia, Greek/Chicken Caesar salads, fries, tiramisu, espresso drinks, soft drinks, and imported-style “BOSNIAN DRINKS.”
- Production/process quotations: Official menu: breakfast steak is “Two hand-cut steaks”; Nature Schnitzel is “hand-cut slices”; Wiener Schnitzel is “hand-cut thin slices”; soup is “Daily fresh prepared chicken noodle soup with vegetables”; Bosnian Pita is “Homemade Phyllo pastry filled with meat, spinach, or cheese.” Official weekday specials include “Slow Cooked Lamb,” veal “slow-cooked with a variety of fresh vegetables,” and beef “slowly cooked in a rich, homemade goulash sauce”; Monday Hunter Schnitzel has “a homemade sauce.” A [Salt Lake Tribune feature](https://archive.sltrib.com/article.php?id=55043824&itype=CMSID) reports: “At Cafe on Main, Delic's makes three different kinds of pitas each day — meat, spinach and cheese — all with phyllo that she makes by hand.” A diner review reproduced by Restaurant Guru calls the accompanying Italian bread “house made,” but the official menu calls it only “fresh Italian bread”; the diner statement is retained as a customer report, not restaurant-confirmed bread production.
- Seasonality/turnover quotations: Official homepage publishes weekday “Daily Specials”: Monday Hunter Schnitzel and Slow Cooked Lamb; Tuesday Mućkalica and Breaded Chicken Special; Wednesday Chicken Goulash, Dolma, and Meatball Schnitzel; Thursday Beef Goulash and Musaka; Friday Mućkalica and Japrak. Monday’s schnitzel is paired with “seasonal vegetables.” The Tribune feature reports that three pita fillings are made “each day” and that the restaurant “sometimes … runs out before the end of the day.” The official site does not attach a calendar date to the weekday-special list; the summer closure notice is current on the 2026-07-15 access date.
- Ingredient/sourcing quotations: Official About Us says the menu uses “quality ingredients,” without naming suppliers or provenance. The official menu names “Grade AA large eggs,” “Premium Angus beef patty,” beef, veal, chicken, whole trout, salmon, tilapia, mozzarella, feta, Greek olives, walnuts, phyllo, and imported Balkan drinks, but no farm, fishery, mill, breed producer, butcher, or other supplier is named.
- Review-text quotations — food/product: Google review reproduced by Restaurant Guru says the Balkan platter had “lovely house made Italian bread that was lite and pillowy, great crumb.” Wanderlog-reproduced Google text calls the veggie omelette “nice texture and great flavor” and says breakfast comes with “nice, fresh Italian bread.” Another says the platter was “loaded with almost every kind of meat on the menu,” calls cevapi and veal kebab favorites, and reports the pita and tufahija were sold out at the end of the day. Restaurant Guru’s indexed current review says the food has “home made flavor.” A Roost Cafe and Bistro aggregation reproduces a diner claiming “real ingredients (not bag junk from Sysco or Nicholas),” but this unsupported customer assertion is not treated as verified sourcing.
- Review-text quotations — service, drinks, price, parking, ambiance: A current Google review reproduced by Restaurant Guru says the server “needs to improve on customer service.” Another dated March 20, 2026 alleges menus were “literally tossed,” butter packets were “cold almost frozen and completely unspreadable,” water was spilled “onto our plates,” and there was “No courtesy, No apology.” Wanderlog reviews describe “fast service,” a patient owner explaining dishes, an “inviting” ambiance, and one of the diner’s “best coffee” experiences. Restaurant Guru calls prices “reasonable”; no specific parking defect evidence was located.
