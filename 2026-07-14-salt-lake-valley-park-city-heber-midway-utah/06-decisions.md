# Phase 6 — Scoring and classification decisions

Decision date: 2026-07-14. Primary orchestrator: `/root`. Every judgment below was made by the primary orchestrator from Phase 5 accepted evidence; workers supplied evidence only.

## Decision conventions

- `S` and `I` are primary estimates; `G` is computed as `sqrt(S×I)`. Presentation tiers: A ≥72, B 64–71.9, C 56–63.9, D below 56 after the R/S gate.
- `R` is literal accepted-source data unless labeled contaminated/conflicting. Ratings were never averaged. `E` is estimated only from accepted product-review text; otherwise it is `unverified`.
- `rating-unconfirmed` is terminal and non-negative. `not-scoreable` means the packet cannot support process depth plus a rating gate; it is neither a low score nor a DQ.
- Home mode uses G/rotation; trip mode uses S with variance notes. Candidates within about 8 G are ties within a tier, not exact ranks.
- A rated survivor is not automatically a practical visit recommendation. No-storefront microbakeries, service-area producers, wholesale identities, and irregular preorder operations remain in the audit but are separated from storefront top lists.
- Evidence references point to accepted original+repair packets in `05-evidence-ledger.md`. Product-only text was never used as process proof.

- Tier A is one craft-score tie band: House of Bread, Hawk & Sparrow, Dough Miner, Leavity, Table X, Le Pain de Charlie, Sidecar, RubySnap, All Purpose, and Fillings & Emulsions span about 9 G points, roughly the rubric's ±8 resolution floor. Storefront access is a separate presentation gate: House of Bread and Le Pain remain in the audit but not the main visit list.
- Tier B is a broad tie across pâtisserie, rare-tradition pastry, bread, pie, cheesecake, and cinnamon rolls. Choose by product and geography.
- Trip mode elevates high-S/low-I traditional or wholesale producers: Red Bicycle, Great Harvest Taylorsville, Vosen's, Stone Ground, and Ballerina Farm. Home mode elevates Dough Miner, RubySnap, Fillings & Emulsions, Momo's, and Pie Party.
- Ballerina Farm's R is contaminated by the farm-store/brand experience. Tulie's aggregate R is café/service-contaminated; baked-goods text clears the gate, but scoped R is an estimate, not a synthesized star average.
